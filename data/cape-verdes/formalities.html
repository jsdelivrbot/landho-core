Cape Verdes - Formalities

Clearance

Report to the Port Captain on arrival at one of the Ports of Entry. Visiting Customs is only required if you have dutiable goods. At subsequent ports, it is only necessary to report to the port captain until leaving the county, when a visit to Immigration is also required.

Visas are no longer necessary for visiting yachts arriving for short stays. Generally, formalities appear to have been simplified in an effort to attract more cruising boats to visit the archipelago. The ship’s papers will be retained by the Authorities, but passports just have to be stamped and are then returned.

There is a fee for clearing out only, irrespective of the length of your stay. This is payable to the Capitania and is separate to any berthing/anchoring fees.

Permits are no longer necessary to visit Fogo and/or Brava islands. However one cannot officially leave the country from those islands and must return to Praia, Sal or Mindelo to collect the ship’s papers and clear out.

Last updated October 2014.

Immigration

May 2015: The Cape Verde government still has restrictions on travel into the country by non-resident foreigners who in the last 30 days have been in Sierra Leone, Guinea or Liberia.

All visitors require a passport valid for at least 6 months.

Citizens of all countries except South Africa, now require a visa. It is advisable to obtain one in advance. Citizen of most European countries can purchase one on arrival, as well as those on yachts making a short visit but this can sometimes be difficult and cause delays.

Any visitor intending to spend longer than 30 days in the islands are advised to purchase a visa in advance.

There are Cape Verdean diplomatic missions in many West European capitals, Boston, Washington, Dakar and Las Palmas de Gran Canaria or one can apply direct to Ministerio de Negocios Estrangeiros, Direccão de Immigracão, Praia, writing in Portuguese.

Applications need two photos and a visa fee is charged.

Last updated May 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared to Customs.

In theory, no tobacco products or Cape Verde currency to be brought into the country.

Last updated October 2014.

Health

Malaria prophylaxis may be necessary in São Tiago. There is tuberculosis throughout the archipelago and cholera has been reported as a risk.

October 2014: The Cape Verde government has announced restrictions on travel into the country by non-resident foreigners who in the last 30 days have been in the countries affected by the Ebola virus. This includes travel from Senegal.

November 2009: Dengue Fever Outbreak

October 2011: CV Radio are reporting cases of Dengue fever in Pria and Mindelo.

Restrictions

Theft appears to be a problem in Mindelo and visitors are advised to employ a local watchman, usually one will be recommended by the port captain.

According to the latest reports, theft appears to be a problem on all the islands. Most vulnerable are loose items left on deck, so the boat should never be left unattended for long periods, and any removable items should be secured.

Pets

Animals must be declared to Customs. A veterinarian health certificate is required. Dogs also require an additional rabies inoculation certificate.
