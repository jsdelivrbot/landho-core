Canary Islands - General Info

Time Zone

UTC

Summer time UTC +1 March to October

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 220 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Note - Type L plugs/outlets may have different pin spacing. The smaller and closer pins are for a rated current of 10 A, the bigger and wider pins are for a rated current of 16 A.

Note about Gas: arrive with propane bottles filled, your gas bottle may not be approved for filling here. See report.

Opening Hours

Banks: 0900-1330 Monday to Friday, some remain open until 1530.

Business: 0800-1600 Monday to Friday (to 1500 in Summer).

Shops: 0900-1300, 1600-2000 Monday to Saturday. Some large department stores 0900-2000.

Government offices: 0800-1500 Monday to Friday, some 0700-1400 in summer.

Money

When paying with a credit card in Gran Canaria, most establishments request your passport or a photo driving license.

Communications

International dialing code for Canary Islands is +34.

Each Spanish network provides coverage of the Canary Islands. This includes the 7 main islands - Gran Canaria, Fuerteventura, Lanzarote, Tenerife, La Palma, Gomera, Hierro - as well as Alegranza, Graciosa, Montaña Clara, Roque del Este, Roque del Oeste und Lobos.

To listen to voicemail just dial 901.

Standard national post is slow, but often poses less of a customs problem if wanting to bring in spare parts. If you are not in too much of a rush, this can be the most economical and reliable option. See the Customs section for further details on shipping in spares to the Canaries.

Transport

All major tourist areas, larger towns and ports are well served by buses. Taxis are plentiful and inexpensive. Town taxis run on meters but for longer trips it is best to ascertain price. Car hire is reasonably priced. There are many flights both scheduled and charter to the international airports. Flights available to mainland Spain and Interisland flights are frequent but they need to be booked well in advance. Ferries between the islands.

Emergencies

Emergency number 112 This number covers health, fire, security and maritime emergencies 24/7 and is answered in Spanish, English, French or German

Emergency Police Station number - 091
Ambulance number - 061
Firefighters number - 080
Hospital Insular - 928 44 40 00
Red Cross - 928 22 22 22





The Spanish Embassy number is 020 7235 5555

The dialling code is the same as the Spanish mainland 0034 (remember to drop the zero from the beginning of the number being called if calling to the UK)

Maritime emergency phone: 900 202 202 (24hrs)

Emergency radio call: VHF ch.16, MW 2182 Khz
