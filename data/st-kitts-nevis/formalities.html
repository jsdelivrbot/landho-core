St Kitts & Nevis - Formalities

Clearance

The SailClear advance web notification service for clearance is now operational. Users previously registered with the old eSeaClear system will find all their details have been carried over to the new system.

There is now a customs office at Zante Marina and this the most convenient place to berth the boat and complete formalities. Clearance is yacht-friendly here and cruisers report 2014 that the SailClear system is fully operational at this marina.

No special clearance for Nevis is now required.

There are similar arrangements for clearing out from Nevis to St Kitts. Arrangements can also be made to visit anchorages outside the Ports of Entry after clearance for another country has been granted.

Last updated June 2014.

Immigration

All visitors require a passport valid for 6 months.

Visa are not required for citizens of many countries including most western and northern European countries, Australia, New Zealand, Canada and the USA for stays of up to 90 days. Check this official St Kitts and Nevis website for a complete list of nationals which require a visa.

To apply for an extension, fill in this application form.

Fees

Latest fees reported are US$12 for Customs and none for Immigration. This covers both islands.

The Port Authority at Port Zante charges $13.

There is an overtime fee on both islands for clearance outside of working hours.

Last updated June 2014.

Restrictions

Be aware that Black coral and most other corals, whether in chunks or in jewellry may not be purchased or collected.

No jet-skis or similar craft may be used by visiting yachts.

Scuba diving may only be done with a local dive shop.

Anchoring is discouraged and a 100 yacht mooring buoys have been laid around the island.
